01 — Problem

Road accidents are silent, sudden, and preventable.

Improve the transport management system for riders with a Smart Helmet — incorporating IoT sensors that monitor, detect, and report incidents during a bike ride, including accident alerts and theft alarms, communicated in real-time to the rider's companion app.

Key Research Findings

🚨 Accident Response Gap

Riders in accidents frequently cannot call for help themselves. 68% of respondents had no fallback mechanism for alerting emergency contacts automatically.

🔒 Theft Blind Spot

89% of surveyed riders had no real-time theft notification system on their vehicles or helmets. Most relied on social media posts after the fact.

📍 Location Anxiety

Families of daily commuters reported significant anxiety about riders' whereabouts, especially on late-night routes or in poor weather conditions.

📱 App Trust Deficit

Riders expressed distrust in overly complex safety apps. They need instant-readable dashboards — especially under stress or in low-visibility conditions.

05 — Design Principles

Designed for moments that matter most.

Six commitments that shaped every screen — from typography to alert logic.

01 ⚡

Instant Clarity

Safety information must be readable in 1 second. High contrast, icon-led, no jargon. Riders glance at their phone — they don't read it.

02 🛡️

Proactive Protection

Don't wait for disaster. Surface warnings before they become critical. Pre-emptive alerts give riders and family peace of mind.

03 🤝

Human in the Loop

Every automated action includes a human cancel window. Riders remain in control — the app assists, never overrides.

04 📶

Offline Resilience

Critical functions work offline. No connectivity? The app queues alerts and fires the moment signal is restored.

05 👪

Family First

The rider's journey is shared — with family who care. Live maps, safe arrival pings, and trip summaries keep loved ones informed without pestering.

06 🎯

Signal, Not Noise

No notification spam. Alerts are tiered by severity. Only true emergencies interrupt — everything else waits for the right moment.

07 — Key Design Decisions

Why we designed it this way.

⏱️

30-Second Override Window

Research showed riders feared false alarms more than actual emergencies. A 30-second countdown with a prominent "I'm okay" button balances immediacy with false alarm prevention. We tested 15s, 30s, and 60s — 30s was the sweet spot.

💡 Insight: During usability testing, 4 of 6 riders said the 60-second timer felt "too slow if I'm bleeding." The 30s version felt urgent but fair.
🎨

Dark UI as Default

Riders often check their phones in full sunlight or at night. A dark-first interface with high-contrast text and icon-led navigation tested 40% faster for screen comprehension in outdoor conditions than a light theme equivalent.

🎯

Safety Score Gamification

A weekly safety score (0–100) based on riding behavior encourages helmet use and safe riding. Early prototype testing showed 72% of users checked their score after every trip — driving consistent app engagement without coercive notifications.

What I Learned

Designing for safety is fundamentally different from designing for convenience. Here are my key takeaways:

Research Finding
Trust is the #1 design challenge — riders won't use a safety app they don't trust 100%. Every edge case, every false alarm destroys trust. The override window isn't a feature; it's a promise.
Design Insight
IoT design is context design — you're designing for riders wearing gloves, in wind, under a helmet. Every tap, every screen state must work in extreme physical conditions, not just in a quiet usability lab.
Process Reflection
Families are stakeholders too — I almost forgot the family member perspective entirely. Including them in research revealed a completely separate set of needs (reassurance, not real-time alerts) that shaped the "family view" feature.
Technical Constraint
Hardware limitations force UX creativity — the BLE range, sensor accuracy thresholds, and battery constraints forced us to design smarter UI around imperfect data, not wait for perfect hardware.
